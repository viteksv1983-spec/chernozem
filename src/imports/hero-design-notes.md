You are a senior UX designer and art director specializing in high-converting landing pages.

Your task is to refine and improve the existing hero section design for a landing page selling chernozem soil in Kyiv.

Important: Do NOT replace the background image. The soil texture is correct and must remain the same.

Focus on improving visual hierarchy, readability, and premium visual quality.

Current background:
Realistic macro texture of dark fertile chernozem soil.

Goals:

1. Improve hero readability
Add a clean dark overlay gradient behind the text to increase contrast.

Recommended overlay:

linear-gradient(
90deg,
rgba(0,0,0,0.75) 0%,
rgba(0,0,0,0.55) 35%,
rgba(0,0,0,0.15) 70%
)

Remove any brown or orange tinted overlays.

2. Improve visual depth

Add a very subtle radial light behind the text block to make the hero feel more premium and layered.

Example:

radial-gradient(
circle at center,
rgba(255,255,255,0.06) 0%,
rgba(0,0,0,0) 60%
)

This effect must be extremely subtle.

3. Adjust background scale

Increase the background image scale slightly (about 10–15%) to make the soil texture feel more powerful and immersive.

4. Maintain natural soil appearance

Do not modify soil colors.
Do not blur the texture.
Do not apply artificial filters.

The soil texture must remain realistic and detailed.

5. Improve typography contrast

Ensure the headline and subheadline remain highly readable over the soil background.

Text should feel strong, premium, and clearly visible.

6. Maintain premium landing page aesthetics

Use:
• strong contrast
• clean gradients
• subtle depth
• minimal visual noise

Avoid:
• heavy filters
• artificial color overlays
• excessive darkening
• replacing the background image

Design goal:

The hero section should feel like a premium product landing page with a rich natural texture background, strong readability, and subtle visual depth.

Act like a senior art director polishing the final hero design before launch.
